Chapter 2.8 Questions

Q1. Write an explanation of abstraction, including at least one example, in your own words.

A1. An abstraction is a simple symbol or concept for something that everyone is able to associate it with. For example with a logo, the logo is not the actual company, but it is a symbol that everyone looks at and knows that it is representing that company.

Q2. Give 2-3 examples of abstractions in everyday life not identified in the lecture.

A2. One example would be a drawing of a tree. Whenever anyone draws a tree, they draw a thick vertical pole and then a cloud at the top. Althought that symbol is not an actual tree, eveyone connects it to a tree as it has similar features. Another example would be the peace sign. The peace sign is not the literal concept of peace, however when someone puts of two fingers others understand that it is the sign for peace as everyone uses it often for that purpose.

Q3. Consider an example of software, a mobile application, or a web site and explain how it is an abstraction

A3. Desmos, a mathematical graphing app is an abstraction as it creates graphs for the user. Each value inputed into the computer requires a lot of operation, however as the user wants to ignore that and focus on the graph, the app hides this calculation and gives the user the graph. As Desmos simplifies line equations and graphs it is considered an abstraction, or a simplified concept.
